Reserve Your Seat

You've been building something real.

A chapter that's grown, a mosad that families depend on, a team you've had to figure out how to lead. And somewhere in the middle of all that growth, it gets harder to stop and ask whether the foundation is keeping up.

That's what this summit is for.

Two days in West Bloomfield with a small group of fellow directors. The conversations most of us don't get to have during the year: about structure, about money, about mission, about what it actually takes to lead a growing Friendship Circle with clarity and confidence.

Because this summit is built around real work and not just sessions you sit through, directors will be asked to complete some preparatory work before arriving. It makes the two days worth it.

Why West Bloomfield

Where it
all began.

This is where Friendship Circle started. A young couple with no building and no budget, just the belief that friendship changes everything. Over thirty years, that belief became LifeTown. Soul Café. A community transformed.

They didn't start there. They grew into it.

We're gathering here intentionally. The directors who built West Bloomfield will be in the room with us. Part of what we're doing together is learning how they did it, not as a template, but as a real conversation about what it takes.

Not comparing your chapter to Michigan, but doing the same kind of intentional work that got Michigan where it is.

What We're Working On

Three sessions.
Real work.

01 / 03

Anchoring in Mission

Led by Rabbi Shais Taub

When a chapter grows, the work gets more complex. It's easy for the original reason you started to quietly fade into the background. This farbreng, rooted in the Rebbe's teachings on shlichus, is about making sure it doesn't.

  • What it means to lead a mosad where every person is seen as a neshama
  • How to make decisions that stay true to who you are, not just what you run
  • How growth can strengthen your identity rather than dilute it

02 / 03

Building Structure That Holds

Led by Mendel Shemtov

Most of us built our chapters by figuring things out as we went. That works until it doesn't. This session is about building the kind of structure that lets you grow without burning out.

  • Clear roles and real accountability
  • Governance that helps you lead rather than slows you down
  • Systems that scale with your chapter, not against it

03 / 03

Fundraising With a Longer View

Led by Tzvi Schectman

Most of us are good at raising money for the next thing. This session is about building something more sustainable.

  • Moving beyond event-to-event pressure
  • Building revenue that's predictable and diversified
  • Fundraising from vision rather than from urgency
